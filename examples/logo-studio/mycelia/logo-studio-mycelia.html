A2 Wordmark

Specimen Label

The brand's restraint encoded in type alone. "mycelia" lowercase in Fraunces (a contemporary reading of Caslon / Mrs Eaves — sharper terminals, modernist optical size), tracked loose, a single hairline rule beneath. Works as pack foil-stamp, page header, or 8mm print.

The Network

The mark in isolation: a hub and twelve filaments contained within a ring, each thread a single hairline, each terminus a graded node. Scales from 8mm foil to full-bleed web. The "only piece of theater" — intended to animate filament-by-filament on load.

C1 Combination

Rhizomorph

The network without the frame. Five hairline filaments wander horizontally, scattered spore-nodes between them — a literal depiction of hyphae spreading under soil. Wordmark holds steady alongside. Feels less like a logo than a diagram torn from a field journal.

C2 Wordmark

Running Filament

The hairline rule reinterpreted as a living filament — a single lichen-sage thread with two terminal nodes, drifting beneath the wordmark rather than ruling it. Category-disruptive: the decoration itself is the concept. Riskier at small sizes.

C3 Symbol

Root System

An asymmetric branching hypha — no containing circle, no mandala. Six organic strokes fork outward and downward like a rhizome under soil, with a single emergent node at top (the fruiting body). The category-breaking option: wilder, less settled, closer to a Victorian plate than a mark.
