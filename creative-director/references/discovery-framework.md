## Discovery Questions

### About the Business

| Question | What It Reveals |
|----------|----------------|
| What is the history of this business? What motivated its creation? | Origin story, founder values, authentic narrative material |
| Why are you looking for this work *now*? | Urgency, crisis, or opportunity — reveals the real trigger |
| What makes you think you need [stated goal]? | The real problem beneath the stated request |
| What are your top 3 priorities needing immediate attention? | Where design effort concentrates |
| What are the short-term and long-term business goals? | Strategic trajectory for the brand to support |
| How would you know these goals have been accomplished? | Forces measurable success criteria |

### About the Audience

| Question | What It Reveals |
|----------|----------------|
| Who is the target audience? (demographics, psychographics, behaviors, geography) | The humans the design serves |
| What do they currently think and feel about the brand/product/category? | Current perception baseline |
| What do they value? What are their aspirations? | Emotional design hooks |
| What problems do they face that this brand addresses? | Functional design hooks |
| What should they think, feel, or do after encountering this brand? | The perception shift design must achieve |

### About the Brand

| Question | What It Reveals |
|----------|----------------|
| If this brand were a person, how would you describe their personality? | Brand personality raw material |
| What 3-5 adjectives describe how you want the brand to feel? | Aesthetic direction seeds |
| What brands do you admire (in any industry) and why? | Aspirational positioning and aesthetic preferences |
| What visual styles, colors, or approaches do you dislike? | Negative space of the design territory |
| Are there existing brand elements that must be preserved? | Constraints and continuity requirements |

### About the Market

| Question | What It Reveals |
|----------|----------------|
| Who are your primary competitors? | Direct competitive set |
| Who are your aspirational peers (brands you'd like to be compared to)? | Aspirational competitive set |
| What makes you different from competitors? | Core differentiators for design to express |
| Where does your brand currently sit in the market (price, quality, accessibility)? | Market position coordinates |

## Competitive Analysis

A visual competitive audit identifies where opportunities exist for differentiation.

### Audit Elements

For each competitor (4+ recommended), catalog:

| Element | What to Examine |
|---------|----------------|
| Logo & Wordmark | Style, complexity, color, personality |
| Color Palette | Primary, secondary, accent colors; warmth, saturation, contrast |
| Typography | Serif/sans-serif, weight, personality, formality |
| Photography Style | Subject, lighting, color treatment, composition |
| Illustration/Iconography | Style, complexity, personality |
| Website Layout | Structure, density, whitespace, hierarchy |
| Messaging Tone | Formal/casual, emotional/rational, jargon level |
| Overall Impression | The 3-5 adjective summary of each competitor's visual brand |

### Analysis Output

Map competitors on the same brand personality spectrums used in Phase 2 of the concept workflow. Identify:

- **Crowded zones** — where most competitors cluster (the visual "sea of sameness")
- **Open territory** — unoccupied spectrum positions representing differentiation opportunities
- **Category conventions** — visual signals that communicate industry belonging (the "assimilation" half of the dual mandate)
- **Aspirational gap** — the visual distance between the peer group and the aspirational group

## Audience Definition

### Audience Profile Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Demographics** | Age, income, education, occupation, location, family status |
| **Psychographics** | Values, attitudes, interests, lifestyle, personality traits |
| **Behaviors** | Purchase habits, brand loyalty, media consumption, technology usage |
| **Aspirations** | What they want to become, achieve, or be associated with |
| **Pain Points** | Frustrations, unmet needs, friction in current solutions |
| **Decision Drivers** | What influences their choices — price, quality, status, convenience, ethics |

### Perception Mapping

| Dimension | Current State | Desired State |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| What they think | [existing perception] | [target perception] |
| What they feel | [existing emotion] | [target emotion] |
| What they do | [current behavior] | [desired behavior] |

The gap between current and desired state is the design opportunity.

## Success Criteria

Translate business goals into measurable design objectives:

| Business Goal | Design Objective | Measurable Outcome |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Increase brand recognition | Distinctive visual identity system | Brand recall in target audience |
| Attract premium customers | Elevated, aspirational visual language | Average transaction value, customer lifetime value |
| Differentiate from competitors | Unique visual territory in competitive landscape | Unprompted brand association with key attributes |
| Build trust with new audience | Credibility signals + approachable aesthetics | Conversion rate, time on site, bounce rate |
| Modernize brand perception | Contemporary visual system replacing dated elements | Perception shift in audience research |

## Brief Template

A complete creative brief contains:

1. **Project Title** — Recognizable name for all stakeholders
2. **Business Overview** — Brand context tailored to this specific project
3. **Project Background** — Purpose, timeline, audience
4. **The Real Problem** — The underlying need driving this work
5. **Objectives** — What success looks like (with measurable criteria)
6. **Target Audience** — Complete profile (demographics, psychographics, behaviors)
7. **Competitive Context** — Market position, competitors, aspirational peers
8. **Brand Personality** — Spectrum positions and adjective clusters
9. **Constraints** — Existing elements, regulatory requirements, budget, timeline
10. **Deliverables** — Specific concept outputs expected
