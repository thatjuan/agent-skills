## Tech / SaaS

**Convention**: Clean, blue-dominant, product-forward, social proof above the fold.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Hero** | Communicates what, who, and what-action within 5 seconds |
| **Imagery** | Real product screenshots outperform abstract illustrations — 27-43% more time on page |
| **Social Proof** | Client logos and quantified results in hero section (2.3x more engagement than below-fold) |
| **Color** | Clean palettes; blues for trust; increasingly orange accents for CTAs |
| **Typography** | Clean sans-serifs (Inter, Manrope, Satoshi); clarity over personality |
| **Layout** | Bento grids for feature showcases; content-first with minimal decoration |
| **Key Element** | Pricing page with obvious path to value (free trial, demo, freemium) |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Most SaaS brands look identical. Bold color choices, distinctive illustration systems, or opinionated typography create memorability without sacrificing usability.

## E-commerce / Retail

**Convention**: Product-first, trust-building, conversion-optimized.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Visual Hierarchy** | Large product name → medium price → smaller specs; hero image for primary campaign |
| **Photography** | Consistent background/lighting; multiple views with zoom; customer photos preferred (77% of shoppers) |
| **Trust** | Security badges (Norton, McAfee, PayPal, Visa) in footer and checkout |
| **Social Proof** | Minimum 5 reviews with visible average near CTA; customer photos increase purchase likelihood 137% |
| **Performance** | 53% of mobile users abandon sites taking >3 seconds |
| **Color** | Brand-aligned; red for urgency/sales events |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Elevated product photography, editorial-style storytelling, and brand-world immersion distinguish premium e-commerce from functional shopping experiences.

## Finance / Fintech

**Convention**: Clarity, security, and credibility over creative gimmicks.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Trust Signals** | Security badges, compliance certifications, partner/investor logos, SSL indicators |
| **Data Visualization** | Charts and dashboards as storytelling tools with contextual animations |
| **Interface** | Minimalist to avoid clutter while remaining data-rich; progressive disclosure |
| **Color** | Blues and greens dominate (18% higher trust ratings); conservative, muted, professional |
| **Typography** | Conservative, high-legibility fonts; generous white space |
| **Layout** | Clean hierarchy; information density managed through progressive disclosure |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Fintech brands (Stripe, Wise, Cash App) have proven that finance can be warm, approachable, and visually interesting without sacrificing credibility. The opportunity is in the space between "serious institution" and "friendly product."

## Healthcare

**Convention**: HIPAA-conscious, accessibility-first, trust-building.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Compliance** | Encryption, HTTPS, multi-factor auth, role-based access visible in design choices |
| **Accessibility** | WCAG 2.1 Level AA mandatory; high contrast; large base font sizes; clear hierarchy |
| **Trust** | Prominent privacy policies in jargon-free language; explicit consent cues |
| **Color** | Blues and greens for trust/calm; high contrast; red reserved for true alerts only |
| **Typography** | Maximum legibility; large base sizes; clear hierarchy for diverse reading abilities |
| **Layout** | Clear site structure; information architecture prioritized over visual flair |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Most healthcare brands feel clinical and impersonal. Warmth through photography, approachable color accents, and human-centered copywriting create care-forward brands.

## Food & Beverage

**Convention**: Appetite-driven, sensory, warmth-forward.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Color Psychology** | Red stimulates appetite; yellow grabs attention; red+yellow combination effective for fast food |
| **Photography** | Natural lighting defines freshness; texture awareness (crisp edges, glossy sauces, bubbles); warm color grading |
| **Philosophy** | "Great food photography doesn't oversell — it invites, creating trust, desire, and connection" |
| **Typography** | More expressive than most industries; hand-drawn/script for artisanal; bold sans-serifs for fast-casual |
| **Imagery** | Every image matches brand personality (rustic, modern, playful, refined) |
| **Atmosphere** | Sensory language and visual warmth are primary brand tools |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Craft/artisanal brands can push into editorial territory with food photography as art rather than product shots. Health-forward brands can break from warm tones into fresh, clean, contemporary palettes.

## Fashion / Luxury

**Convention**: Whispered confidence. "Luxury doesn't scream."

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **White Space** | Strategic breathing room creates exclusivity; products resonate more with space |
| **Photography** | Editorial-style, not e-commerce; close-up textures; motion graphics and video loops |
| **Typography** | 1-2 intentional font families; generous line spacing; serifs/display fonts for authority |
| **Color** | Monochromatic or neutral; black, white, gold, deep navy; minimal accent colors |
| **UX** | Frictionless but unhurried; experience over efficiency |
| **Storytelling** | Heritage, craftsmanship, exclusivity embedded through micro-copy and hover interactions |
| **Cross-Channel** | Visual language uniform across every touchpoint |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Emerging luxury brands break convention with bold color, maximalist layouts, and accessible language — redefining luxury for younger audiences without losing aspiration.

## Education / EdTech

**Convention**: Approachable, multi-audience, learning-focused.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Trust** | 75% of credibility from visual design; visitors form trustworthiness opinions in ~50ms |
| **Multi-User** | Serves educators, administrators, and learners — each with distinct needs |
| **Engagement** | Gamification: streaks, badges, leaderboards, character-led motivation |
| **Personalization** | Customizable profiles, learning paths, interface themes |
| **Accessibility** | Closed captions, transcripts, WCAG compliance; equitable learning |
| **Color** | Approachable, friendly palettes; blues for trust, greens for growth |
| **Typography** | High legibility; clear hierarchy for instructional content; friendly but professional |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Personality-driven brands (Duolingo model) with character, humor, and distinctive illustration systems stand far apart from institutional education aesthetics.

## Non-profit / Cause

**Convention**: Story-driven, emotionally activating, action-oriented.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Storytelling** | Storytelling campaigns generate 50% more donations than data-only; 45% donor retention vs. 27% |
| **Identifiable Victim** | Single-person narratives outperform statistical presentations |
| **Visual Impact** | 33% of donors say clear visuals increase donation likelihood; short videos increase conversions 86% |
| **Pattern** | Compelling hook (image, video, testimonial) → emotional journey → clear CTA |
| **Color** | Warm, empathetic tones; cause-aligned colors; avoid cold corporate palettes |
| **Photography** | Real people, real situations; authentic over polished; connection through faces |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Design-forward non-profits (charity: water, ACLU) prove that cause-driven brands can be visually sophisticated. The opportunity is combining emotional power with design craft.

## Entertainment / Media

**Convention**: Immersive, content-forward, dark-mode-default.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Dark Mode** | Reduces eye fatigue; highlights content; saves OLED battery; sleek, modern feel |
| **Typography** | Bold, attention-seeking fonts for content hierarchy |
| **Visuals** | Dynamic video backgrounds, 3D elements, interactive animations; scroll-triggered |
| **Layout** | Card-based grids; horizontal scrolling carousels; content-first with minimal chrome |
| **Color** | High contrast against dark backgrounds; vibrant accents; brand-specific palettes |
| **Motion** | Purposeful animation supporting narrative; hover reveals; smooth transitions |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: While most entertainment brands are dark and dense, editorial-style entertainment brands with generous whitespace and typographic hierarchy create distinction.

## Real Estate

**Convention**: Photography-driven, lifestyle-forward, search-functional.

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Hero** | Immediately communicates who, where, and specialty; often includes search |
| **Photography** | Over 60% of viewer time goes to photos; consistent color grading; lifestyle alongside property |
| **Luxury Approach** | Properties as lifestyle stories, not data sheets; cinematic presentations |
| **Trust** | Agent headshots, testimonials, award badges, neighborhood imagery |
| **Layout** | Vertical gallery formats; full-bleed hero imagery; clean listing grids |
| **Search** | Prominent, intuitive property search tools above the fold |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Most real estate sites are template-driven. Custom editorial design, neighborhood storytelling, and cinematic property presentations create premium positioning.

## Professional Services

**Convention**: Restraint as credibility. "Typography, spacing, photography, and microcopy are credibility tools, not decoration."

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Principle** | Specific about expertise, real evidence, clean hierarchy, predictable navigation |
| **Authority** | Certifications, awards, practice area specializations at top of page; thought leadership |
| **Color** | Conservative: deep navy, charcoal, burgundy, forest green; minimal bright accents |
| **Typography** | Traditional serifs for headings (authority); clean sans-serifs for body; generous spacing |
| **Photography** | Professional headshots; office environments; avoid generic stock |
| **Audience** | Sophisticated — the most credible approach is curated and restrained |

**Differentiation Opportunity**: Firms that inject personality while maintaining authority (distinctive color accent, custom illustration, bold typographic choices) stand apart from the navy-and-serif default.
