## Brand Archetypes

The 12 Jungian archetypes with their visual characteristics. Each archetype implies a distinct visual language for logo construction.

| Archetype | Core Drive | Shapes | Colors | Typography |
|-----------|-----------|--------|--------|------------|
| **Innocent** | Safety, simplicity | Circles, soft edges | White, sky blue, pastels | Clean sans-serif, light weight |
| **Explorer** | Freedom, discovery | Arrows, open paths, horizons | Earth tones, forest green, tan | Rugged sans-serif, wide tracking |
| **Sage** | Knowledge, truth | Squares, books, symmetry | Navy, deep blue, gray | Classic serifs, medium weight |
| **Hero** | Mastery, courage | Shields, chevrons, bold angles | Red, black, gold | Heavy sans-serif, uppercase |
| **Outlaw** | Liberation, disruption | Jagged edges, broken shapes | Black, red, dark palette | Distressed, unconventional display |
| **Magician** | Transformation | Stars, spirals, gradients | Purple, iridescent, deep blue | Elegant serifs or custom lettering |
| **Regular Guy** | Belonging | Circles, handshake motifs | Brown, warm neutrals, blue | Friendly rounded sans-serif |
| **Lover** | Intimacy, passion | Hearts, curves, flowing forms | Red, pink, burgundy, gold | Script, italic serifs |
| **Jester** | Enjoyment, play | Irregular shapes, bouncing forms | Bright multicolor, yellow, orange | Playful display, varied weights |
| **Caregiver** | Service, protection | Embracing curves, hands, hearts | Soft blue, green, warm tones | Rounded sans-serif, approachable |
| **Creator** | Innovation, vision | Abstract geometry, asymmetry | Vibrant or monochrome contrasts | Custom lettering, distinctive display |
| **Ruler** | Control, authority | Crests, crowns, strong geometry | Black, gold, navy, burgundy | Refined serifs, capitals, tight tracking |

### Archetype Mapping Method

The brand's archetype is determined by its emotional promise — not by what it sells but by how it makes people feel.

Example mappings:
- A financial advisor could be **Sage** (wisdom), **Caregiver** (protection), or **Ruler** (control) depending on positioning
- A fitness brand could be **Hero** (mastery), **Explorer** (freedom), or **Outlaw** (breaking limits)
- A food brand could be **Caregiver** (nourishment), **Jester** (fun), or **Creator** (craft)

### Adjacent Archetype Blending

Archetypes form natural clusters:

| Cluster | Archetypes | Shared Drive |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| Mastery | Hero, Magician, Ruler | Power and transformation |
| Belonging | Regular Guy, Lover, Jester | Connection and enjoyment |
| Independence | Innocent, Explorer, Sage | Fulfillment through understanding |
| Stability | Caregiver, Creator, Ruler | Structure and care |

Direction B concepts blend the primary archetype with one from an adjacent cluster.

## Color Psychology

### Primary Color Associations

| Color | Communicates | Common Industries | SVG Values |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Blue** | Trust, competence, calm | Finance, tech, healthcare | `#0071e3`, `#2563eb`, `#1e3a5f` |
| **Red** | Energy, urgency, passion | Food, retail, entertainment | `#e34040`, `#dc2626`, `#991b1b` |
| **Black** | Luxury, authority, sophistication | Fashion, premium, legal | `#1d1d1f`, `#0f172a` |
| **Green** | Growth, health, sustainability | Organic, finance, environment | `#34c759`, `#16a34a`, `#166534` |
| **Yellow/Orange** | Optimism, warmth, accessibility | Youth, food, budget | `#f59e0b`, `#ea580c`, `#f97316` |
| **Purple** | Creativity, premium, wisdom | Beauty, spiritual, innovation | `#8b5cf6`, `#7c3aed`, `#6d28d9` |
| **White** | Purity, simplicity, modernity | Tech, healthcare, minimalist | `#ffffff`, `#f5f5f7` |

### Differentiation Through Color

When competitors cluster around the same palette (e.g., every fintech uses blue), a deliberate break creates instant category differentiation. The risk-reward equation: breaking color convention stands out but requires stronger brand equity to support.

### Combination Strategies

| Strategy | Effect | Example |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Complementary (opposite) | Energy, contrast | Blue + orange |
| Analogous (adjacent) | Harmony, cohesion | Blue + teal + green |
| Monochromatic | Sophistication, focus | Navy + medium blue + light blue |
| Triadic | Vibrance, balance | Red + blue + yellow |

Logo color palettes are limited to 2-3 colors maximum for versatility.

## Shape Psychology

### Shape Associations

| Shape | Communicates | Use For |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Circles / Ovals** | Community, unity, warmth, continuity | Approachable, inclusive brands |
| **Squares / Rectangles** | Stability, reliability, structure | Institutions, engineering, professional services |
| **Triangles (up)** | Aspiration, growth, power | Achievement-oriented brands |
| **Triangles (right)** | Forward motion, progress | Innovation, media, momentum brands |
| **Organic / Freeform** | Creativity, naturalness, humanity | Artisanal, wellness, creative brands |
| **Angular / Pointed** | Technical, aggressive, precise | Masculine-coded, performance brands |
| **Rounded / Soft** | Friendly, accessible, warm | Feminine-coded, consumer brands |

### Shape-Archetype Alignment

| Archetype | Primary Shapes | Construction Style |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Hero / Ruler | Shields, chevrons, strong rectangles | Bold stroke, sharp corners |
| Sage / Creator | Squares, abstract geometry | Precise, mathematical |
| Caregiver / Lover | Curves, embracing forms | Flowing, organic |
| Explorer / Outlaw | Open paths, arrows, broken forms | Dynamic, asymmetric |
| Innocent / Regular Guy | Circles, simple geometry | Clean, balanced |
| Jester / Magician | Irregular, spirals, stars | Playful, energetic |

## Typography Psychology

### Type Category Associations

| Category | Communicates | Use For |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Serif** | Tradition, credibility, authority | Law, publishing, luxury, established brands |
| **Sans-serif** | Modern, clean, accessible | Tech, startups, contemporary brands |
| **Geometric sans** | Precision, innovation, objectivity | Tech, science, architecture |
| **Humanist sans** | Warmth, approachability, clarity | Consumer brands, education, healthcare |
| **Script** | Elegance, personal touch, creativity | Fashion, beauty, boutique services |
| **Slab serif** | Strength, confidence, groundedness | Industrial, editorial, lifestyle |
| **Display / Custom** | Bold personality, distinctive presence | Entertainment, food, cultural brands |

### Typographic Modifiers

| Property | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Heavy weight** | Confidence, authority, dominance |
| **Light weight** | Elegance, refinement, minimalism |
| **Wide tracking** | Luxury, breathing room, prestige |
| **Tight tracking** | Urgency, density, tech |
| **Uppercase** | Authority, strength, announcement |
| **Lowercase** | Approachability, friendliness, informality |
| **Custom lettering** | Uniqueness, ownable asset (at cost of complexity) |

## Market Positioning

### Competitive Audit Process

1. Collect the top 10-15 competitor logos
2. Map them on a 2×2 matrix (Traditional↔Modern × Playful↔Serious)
3. Identify empty quadrants — that is the visual opportunity space
4. Concepts target the empty space while maintaining category recognition

### Differentiation Levers

Change at least **two** of these from category convention to stand apart, while keeping **one** for category recognition:

1. Color palette
2. Shape language
3. Typography weight/style
4. Level of abstraction
5. Use of negative space

### Resonance Check

The brand resonance question: "If this brand were a person, who would it be?"

If the answer aligns with the target customer's aspirations (not their current state, but who they want to become), the concept has market fit.
