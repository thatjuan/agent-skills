## Duration Conventions

| Duration | Use | Beat Count | Frame Count | Hook Window | V/O Word Budget |
|----------|-----|-----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **6s bumper** | YouTube non-skip, OOH digital | 2–3 | 2–3 | Frame 1 | 12–15 words max |
| **15s** | Social, pre-roll, broadcast cutdown | 4–6 | 4–6 | First 2s | 30–35 words |
| **30s** | Broadcast workhorse, pre-roll | 7–10 | 7–10 | First 3s | 60–70 words |
| **60s** | Brand, prestige broadcast, hero pre-roll | 12–18 | 12–18 | First 5s | 120–140 words |
| **90s** | Cinema, hero brand film | 18–25 | 18–25 | First 5s | 180–210 words |
| **2:00 hero film** | Brand campaign launch, manifesto | 20–35 | 20–35 | First 8s | 240–280 words |
| **3:00–6:00 branded content** | YouTube long-form, founder story, doc-style | 30–60 | 30–60 | First 15s | Pace dialogue/V/O naturally |

**Read pace rule of thumb:** ~2.5 words/second comfortable broadcast V/O. Conversational dialogue runs faster (3–3.5 wps); somber/cinematic V/O runs slower (1.8–2.2 wps).

## Platform Conventions

### TV / Broadcast / OTT (CTV)

- 16:9. 30s and 15s dominate.
- Brand reveal acceptable late (last 5s). Audience tolerates slower builds.
- Mandatories: legal supers in last 3s, sound-off-friendly hero.
- End frame: pack shot + tagline + URL + sonic logo, ~3s held.

### YouTube — Skippable Pre-Roll (TrueView)

- Hook in first 5s before skip button. Brand and SMP land before second 5.
- 15s and 30s most common; 60s+ for brand stories.
- Companion banner / end card overlay.

### YouTube — Non-Skippable / Bumper

- 6s bumper = single-image, single-line discipline. One idea, one twist, one logo.
- 15s non-skippable: hook–build–payoff–logo. No fat.

### Instagram / TikTok / Reels / Shorts (Vertical Social)

- 9:16. 15s sweet spot, 6–60s viable.
- Hook in first 1.5s — pattern interrupt, motion, or face on camera.
- Sound-on platform (TikTok/Reels), but always design captions for sound-off scrollers.
- "Native" register beats polish — face-to-camera, handheld, on-screen text dominant.
- Hook formats: bold claim, question, contrarian take, before/after, demo opener, cold open with dialogue.

### Facebook / Instagram Feed

- 1:1 or 4:5. 6–15s sweet spot.
- Captions on-screen by default. ~85% sound-off historically.
- First frame must work as a still — it's the autoplay poster.

### LinkedIn

- 1:1 or 16:9. 30–90s. Founder-led, B2B, recruiting.
- Captions mandatory. Slower hook acceptable (8–10s).

### X / Twitter

- 16:9 or 1:1. 6–30s. Punchy, captioned.

### Cinema

- 16:9 / 2.39:1 (scope). 30–90s. Premium production expected.
- Audience is captive — slower openings rewarded. Sound-on guaranteed.

### OOH Digital (Billboard, Transit, In-Store)

- No sound. Loops every 6–15s. Single visual idea.
- Type and pack-shot must be legible at distance.

### Connected TV / Hulu / Streaming

- 15s and 30s. Sound-on. Often non-skippable.
- Treat as broadcast but expect a younger, more attention-fragmented viewer.

## Format Archetypes

### Hero Anthem / Manifesto

- 60–90s brand film. V/O-led, montage, score-forward.
- Structure: state of the world → tension → conviction → call → transformation.
- Risk: cliché manifesto. Earn it with specificity and a real point of view (Hey Whipple).

### Narrative Spot

- 30–60s. Character-driven, cinematic, story over slogan.
- Structure: Save the Cat compressed or Pixar Story Spine.
- The brand earns its place by enabling the resolution.

### Demo / Product Hero

- 15–30s. Show, not tell. Ogilvy's house style.
- Structure: setup → demonstration → benefit reveal → end frame.
- Typically: insert-heavy, hand-action, screen-replacement, before/after.

### Vignette Reel / Anthology

- 30–60s. Multiple micro-stories cut together under one V/O or score.
- Structure: 4–8 vignettes, each 3–6s, building to a thematic payoff.
- Useful for breadth of audience, breadth of use cases.

### Testimonial / UGC

- 15–60s. Real customer or actor-as-customer face-to-camera.
- Structure: hook line → problem → discovery → proof → recommendation.
- Native register. Captions mandatory.

### Founder Story / Origin

- 60s–3min. Founder face-to-camera + B-roll.
- Structure: "Why I Am Here" + "Values in Action" (Annette Simmons).

### Comparative / Two-World

- 15–30s. Before/After, Them/Us, Old-Way/New-Way.
- Structure: world A → reveal world B → show the gap.

### Mnemonic / Anthem-Lite

- 6–15s. A repeatable visual + sonic signature. Built for frequency.
- Structure: cold open hook → brand mnemonic → end card.

### Episodic / Serialized

- 3–5 connected spots in a campaign. Recurring cast, escalating premise.
- Each ep = self-contained payoff + thread to the next.

### Branded Documentary

- 2–6min. Doc-style, real subjects, low V/O, score-led.
- Structure: classic doc — character intro → tension → journey → reflection.

## Aspect Ratios & Safe Zones

| Ratio | Use |
|-------|-----|
| **16:9** | TV, YouTube horizontal, OTT, cinema |
| **9:16** | TikTok, IG Reels, YouTube Shorts, Snapchat |
| **1:1** | Facebook/IG feed, Twitter, LinkedIn feed |
| **4:5** | IG/FB feed (more vertical real estate without going full vertical) |
| **2.39:1** | Cinematic / scope, prestige brand films |

**Safe zone rule:** keep type and key action within central 80% to survive platform UI overlays (caption bars, like/share rails, scrubbers, captions auto-generated by platform).

## Asset Suite

A campaign rarely ships a single cut. Plan from the start for the full suite:

- **Hero cut** — flagship duration (30s or 60s)
- **Cutdowns** — 15s, 6s
- **Vertical adaptations** — 9:16, 1:1, 4:5
- **Bumpers / mnemonics** — 6s
- **Stills** — frame grabs or styled stills for static placements
- **GIFs / loops** — 2–4s, often the mnemonic moment
- **Subtitled / no-V/O cuts** — for sound-off platforms
- **Localized cuts** — language tracks, on-screen type swaps

When storyboarding, flag which frames are anchor frames for which cutdown. The 6s often lives inside the 30s as 2 specific frames — design for that.
